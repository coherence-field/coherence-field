About

Coherence Field Theory is a structural science that examines how systems function based on their underlying architecture and the conditions they operate within. It provides a non‑narrative framework for understanding coherence, drift, and system behavior across contexts.

The discipline studies how coherence forms, stabilizes, and collapses, identifying the architectural forces that govern alignment, distortion, and field compatibility. Its focus is on structural mechanics rather than interpretation or domain‑specific explanation.

CFT isolates the conditions under which systems maintain integrity or lose it through drift, offering a clear architectural lens for analyzing stability, interaction, and environmental influence.
