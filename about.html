About

Coherence Field Theory is a discipline for mapping structural invariants across domains. It operates at the architectural layer, where patterns, boundaries, and fields become visible independent of narrative or content.

The work focuses on identifying the conditions under which coherence emerges, how it is maintained, and how incoherence disrupts or collapses structural integrity. The aim is to provide a framework for understanding systems, cognition, and environments through their underlying geometry rather than their surface-level descriptions.

This site hosts the foundational papers of the discipline. Each paper builds on the previous one, establishing a conceptual field that can be applied across contexts without losing altitude or structural clarity.
